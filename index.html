Why this exists
Ceramic chemistry is good 1920s science, decent 1990s software, and almost no 21st-century thinking. The Stull chart — the field's main visual tool — was published in 1922. The largest open recipe database has roughly 12,000 usable rows. Nobody tracks firing curves rigorously. Nobody links predicted surface outcomes to measurable physical results.

RL Ventures is one person working at that intersection: a potter who writes verification software, a programmer who fires kilns. The patent is about the bridge between the two. The work is the proof that both sides exist in the same hands. Ground the abstract — ideas mean nothing until they touch reality.

Structural gaps in the field. We name them publicly because naming what's missing is the first step of verification. State is updated as work progresses.

The Molar Weight Problem
Material chemistry shifts over decades. Nobody tracks era. A 1965 feldspar is not a 2025 feldspar. Working.
Working
02
The Stull Chart Is a Slice
A 2D projection through a high-dimensional space. The full space has never been mapped publicly. Working — Stull Atlas explorer extends it.
Working
03
Firing Curves Are Invisible
No widespread heatwork accounting. Cone numbers compress hours of thermal history into one integer. Working — Orton cone simulator.
Working
04
Data Is Scattered
~340K entries across community sites, ~12K usable. No central, versioned, license-clean corpus. ML pipeline live on 9,815 recipes with image embeddings.
Working
05
The Surface-Chemistry Gap
UMF (unity molecular formula) says nothing about surface quality. Carbon trap, crystal, oil spot — invisible to the chart. Open. First probes trained.
Open

Ground Truth Missing
Predictions go unverified. The patent thesis — link predicted outcomes to measured physical results — addresses this directly.
